The Physics

This simulation uses the Lattice Boltzmann Method (LBM) — instead of solving the Navier-Stokes equations directly, it models the fluid as particles streaming and colliding on a lattice grid.

Each cell tracks 9 distribution functions (D2Q9) representing the probability of finding a particle moving in each direction. At every step:

  1. Stream — distributions shift to neighboring cells
  2. Collide — distributions relax toward equilibrium (BGK operator)
  3. Bounce-back — distributions reflect off solid boundaries

The Reynolds number Re = UL/ν controls the flow regime. Low Re means viscosity dominates (laminar). High Re means inertia dominates (turbulent).
